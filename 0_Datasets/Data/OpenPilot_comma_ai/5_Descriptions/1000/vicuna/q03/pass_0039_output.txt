The on-off ramp is a type of road that allows vehicles to enter or exit a controlled-access highway, such as a freeway or motorway. On-off ramps are typically located at interchanges, where two or more highways meet. They are designed to provide a smooth and safe transition for vehicles moving between the highways and the local road network.

On-off ramps are essential for the safe and efficient flow of traffic on controlled-access highways. They help to reduce congestion and delays by allowing vehicles to enter and exit the highway smoothly and quickly, without having to merge with traffic on the mainline of the highway. On-off ramps also help to reduce the risk of accidents by providing a separate, controlled environment for vehicles to enter and exit the highway.

Overall, on-off ramps play a crucial role in the design and operation of controlled-access highways, helping to ensure the safe and efficient movement of traffic.
